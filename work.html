How I work

Every project is unique

Each project's specific challenges require a custom process. The general steps to solve problems remain however similar.

  • Define the problem, usually with stakeholders
  • Conduct research to understand the users
  • Match business goals with user needs
  • Collaborative Ideation phase
  • Information architecture
  • Iterative wireframing, prototyping and testing
  • Making sure the solution is well implemented
  • Tracking the outcome and optimizing continuously

Each step can involve diverse research and design methods, but can be efficiently presented as a limited set of deliverables.

Defining the problem

I usually define the process with stakeholders based on the goals, current state, time frame and budget of the project to give you the best value for your money.

I can start from a requirements document to propose a UX strategy or gather requirements through interviews, workshops and reviews of existing content or competitor reviews.

Understand the user through personas

Personas are a synthetic presentation of insights about users uncovered through user research. It helps share a common, realistic vision of users across stakeholders and is a useful tool for ideations and iteration reviews.

Matching business goals with user needs in journey maps

Journey maps tell the story of users engagement with the company acrosss touchpoints. It communicates user actions, motivations, questions and barriers to identify opportunities and bridge gaps between channels.

Collaborative Ideation workshops

I lead collaborative ideation workshops to quickly explore diverse solutions to the problem and define a concept. Typical outputs from these workshops can be living post-it walls, a backlog, user flow diagramms, a content inventory or interface sketches.

Information architecture

Inventory content, classify and create a hierarchy to lay the foundations of an efficient navigation and easy access to content.

I create mindmaps to make ideation workshop output concrete when generating new contnet. I find mind maps, like physical cards, make content more tangible and encourages stakeholders to experiment more.

I use spreadsheets when I need to inventory existing content precisely. This creates an exhaustive and living document. I use it to tracks all content from the old or early version to final, improved design. I also include information on associated wireframes, budgeting information and responsabilities for example. My clients often use this further to add SEO keywords, meta data, specifications or other requirements for their content strategy.

Once the content is classified, a tree view helps to get a big picture idea of the navigation and discuss the high level categories.

I use X-Mind to quickly switch the display of content between the spreadsheet and tree view.

I like to combine my expert approach with collaborative workshops to take in stakeholder's expertise on their content. Involving end-users through card sorting and tree testing ensures the content structure is natural for the user and allows them to complete tasks efficiently.

Iterative wireframing, prototyping and testing

Paper prototyping

Thumbnail design and paper prototypes are efficient and inexpensive. What's more anyone can make them and they're just enough to generate ideas quickly and test them.

Wireframing

As the project moves forward, paper prototypes gain a higher definition as I refine them with other experts.

Grayscale wireframes are ideas to progressively add detail to a design and focusing stakeholder feedback on functionnality without distractions from the look and feel early in the project.

As the project matures, wireframes become more detailed. I add some interactivity: click to navigate from one page to another, display lightboxes, menu interactions, sliders or accordions and test the first user flows.

Working with visual designers from the start, wireframes progressively converge towards to final asthetics of the interfaces before passing them on to the visual designer.

When a visual design is complete, I can create highly interactive prototypes and simulate complex behavior for user testing before any code is written by the developpers.

Interactive prototypes

My highly interactive prototypes can let users navigate a site as if it were real, actually filter and sort through lists, drag andd rop interactions and even use conditions to display one version or another, for example simulating a login status. This is possible thanks to my favorite tool, Axure.

Mockups

I am no art director, but I do know how to use sketch to create a clean design and build upon an existing styleguide. I can produce and export all visual assets required for smaller scale projects.

Implementation of the designed solutions

Both a unique document and face to face communication garantees to efficiently convey the status and rationale behind design decisions to stakeholders, developers and other team members. Following up and collaborating with the team in the long term is one of the most rewarding aspects of a project.
